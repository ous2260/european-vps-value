## Why European VPS Location Matters More Than You Think

Here's the thing people skip over when they're just chasing the lowest price: server location has a direct, measurable impact on user experience.

A server sitting in Frankfurt or Amsterdam will typically deliver **10–30ms latency** to visitors across Western Europe. Route that same traffic through a US-based server and you're looking at 80–150ms. For a website, that difference is invisible to you and obvious to your users. For an API or real-time app, it's the difference between feeling snappy and feeling broken.

Beyond speed, there are compliance reasons to stay in Europe. GDPR requires that personal data from EU citizens be handled under appropriate safeguards, and hosting your infrastructure inside the EU is the cleanest way to tick that box without expensive legal gymnastics.

Then there's connectivity quality. The best European data centers peer with major Internet Exchanges — AMS-IX in Amsterdam, DE-CIX in Frankfurt, LINX in London. A VPS at a well-connected location isn't just geographically closer to your users, it's routed more intelligently.

## What "Cheap" Actually Means in the European VPS Market

The cheap European VPS market in 2026 starts around **€2–$3 per month** for entry-level plans — typically 1 vCPU, 512 MB to 1 GB RAM, 10–20 GB SSD storage, and a few hundred GB of monthly transfer.

What separates providers at this price point isn't usually RAM or storage (those are commoditized). The real differentiators are:

- **CPU clock speed** — this is where most budget providers hide the ball. Running you at 2.2–2.4 GHz and hoping you don't notice.
- **Storage type** — NVMe vs. regular SSD vs. HDD. The gap between NVMe and spinning disk at this price tier is enormous.
- **Network quality and peering** — not all "European" servers are equally connected.
- **What's included** — backups, DDoS protection, IPv6, firewall, control panel. These add up fast if you're paying à la carte.
- **Billing surprises** — egress fees, bandwidth overage charges, CPU burst fees. The "cheap" price can balloon quickly.
